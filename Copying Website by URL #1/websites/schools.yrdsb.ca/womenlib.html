PEOPLE & EVENTS > WORLD AT WAR > Women's Liberation Movement

What?
• 1960s: women protested for changes in politics, life choices, employment practices
• women had little say in politics, few held government jobs, few were doctors
• most jobs women held jobs that paid less and were less prestigious


Historical Significance
• "mainstream" feminists: focused on changing laws, publicizing their cause
• "radical" feminists: believed in stronger actions for change, felt men wouldn't give up their jobs to them willingly
• 1967: Royal Commission on the Status of Women formed; worked to "ensure equal opportunities for women & men in all aspects of Canadian society"


Florence Bird, headed establishment of Royal Commission on the Status of Women
