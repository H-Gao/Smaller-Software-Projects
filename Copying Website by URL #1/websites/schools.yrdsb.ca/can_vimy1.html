What?
• brutal trench warfare of the Western Front
• taking of the Vimy Ridge in the hands of the Canadian Corps
• every stage of the attack rehearsed to the last detail under the command of Canadian Arthur Currie



Historical Significance
• a high point of Canadian military achievement in WW1
• Canadians gained a reputation as tough, effective and courageous fighters
• method worked, and the Canadians took Vimy Ridge successfully, proving the phrase that Currie used "Through preparation must lead to success. Neglect nothing."
• showed the world that Canadians were capable of devising and carrying out a well-planned and successful attack
