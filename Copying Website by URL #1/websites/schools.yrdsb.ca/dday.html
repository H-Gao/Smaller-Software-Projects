PEOPLE & EVENTS > WORLD AT WAR > D-Day

What?
• June 6, 1944; Day of Deliverance
• Allied invasion of Europe to regain France, in Normandy Beaches (northern France)
• Allies created a decoy away from true site of invasion with false radio signals, inflatable tanks
• Allies attacked from skies (paratroopers, bombings) and from warships


Historical Significance
• was Canada's largest military operation, Royal Canadian Navy and Royal Canadian Air Force
• Canadian troops at Juno beach faced underwater obstacles, landmines, barbed wire, machine gun fire from Germans
• at end of day, Canada was the only Allied force to meet objectives
• 335 Canadians killed, 739 injured
