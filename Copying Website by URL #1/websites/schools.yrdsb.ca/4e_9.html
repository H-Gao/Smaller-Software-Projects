PEOPLE > TURN OF THE CENTURY > The Nazi Soviet Pact

What?
• An agreement made by Germany and Soviet Union on Poland
• By signing the pact, both sides promised not to go with war on each other
• Secretly agreed to divide Poland between them


Historical Significance
• Both sides knew that pact would eventually shatter
• For Soviet Union, pact meant that they had plenty of time to build up its army and prepare for inevitable war with Germany
• For Germany it meant that they could first conquer Western Europe before turning its guns on Soviet Union
• Pact allowed Hitler to take Poland on Sept. 1, 1939
• Unofficial declaration of WW2
