PEOPLE & EVENTS > WORLD AT WAR > Constitution Act 1982 and Charter of Rights and Freedoms

What?
• a new constitution deal was made to consider Quebec's needs
• the constitution had been made without Quebec's consent, Quebec was left out of the final meeting
• the agreement, 1) gave Canada the power to amend the constitution by itself, 2) changes could be made to the constitution if the federal government and 7 provinces (with 50% of Canada's population) agreed, 3) included a Charter of Rights and Freedoms


Historical Significance
• British North American Act had been brought home, officially changed and renamed the Constitution Act 1982
• the Constitution Act recognized Canada as a fully independent nation
• the Charter of Rights and Freedoms guaranteed Canadians democratic, civil and legal rights
• the Charter included the following sections, fundamental freedoms, legal rights, equality rights, language rights, Aboriginal rights
