PEOPLE & EVENTS > WORLD AT WAR > Quiet Revolution

What?
• changes made under Jean Lesage's government named Quiet Revolution, 1960-1966
• promises made to improve economic & social standards of people in Quebec, and to win greater recognition for all French speaking people & give them greater control over their own futures
• government took control of hydroelectric companies to give Quebeckers more voice in their economic futures


Historical Significance
• "on est capable,"-"we can do it!"
• another slogan: "Maîtres chez nous," means "Masters in our own house," encouraged French-speaking Quebeckers to take control of Quebec and prevent it from being pushed over by English Canadians
• government wanted to give French Canadians more control over Quebec's affairs
• government also began to replace programs previously run by the Church: hospital insurance, pension schemes, beginnings of medicare
