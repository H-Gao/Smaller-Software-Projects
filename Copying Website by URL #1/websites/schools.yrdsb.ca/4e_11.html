PEOPLE > TURN OF THE CENTURY > War Measures Act

What?
• As result of Act, most Japanese Canadians were removed from coastal British Columbia to internment camps or to work in sugar beet fields in Alberta
• Shameful treatment of Japanese occurred during the war, and after it, many were deported back to Japan


Historical Significance
• Valuable contributions from Japanese-Canadians made during war years by providing vital labour in sugar beet fields
• Chronic shortage of manual labour threatened harvest
• Created harsh relations between Japanese and Canadians
• Canadian government did not formally apologize until 1988 when Brian Mulroney offered $20 000 to every survivor of Japanese internment
